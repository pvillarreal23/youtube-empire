## Email 4: Value Bomb
**Trigger:** Day 7
**Subject:** The "layer cake" method for getting expert-level AI output
**Preview text:** One prompt isn't enough. Here's how to stack them for results that actually impress you.

---

Hey — Pedro here.

Today I want to teach you something that took me weeks to figure out on my own. It's the single biggest unlock I've found for getting genuinely good work out of AI.

I call it the **layer cake method.** (Not a fancy name. But it works.)

**The problem most people have:**

You write one prompt. You get one response. It's... okay. Maybe 60% of what you wanted. So you either accept mediocre output or give up and do it yourself.

**The fix: stop trying to do everything in one prompt.**

Instead, break your task into layers. Each prompt builds on the last. Here's how it works in practice.

**Example: writing a marketing email for a product launch**

**Layer 1 — Context dump:**
> "I'm launching a new [product]. My target audience is [who]. The main benefit is [what]. The launch date is [when]. My brand voice is [how you sound]. Acknowledge this context and confirm you understand before we start."

Don't ask it to write anything yet. Just set the table.

**Layer 2 — Structure:**
> "Now outline 3 different approaches for a launch email. For each: the angle, the emotional hook, and the CTA. Don't write the emails yet — just the strategic framework."

Now you get to choose the direction before any writing happens.

**Layer 3 — Draft:**
> "Write the email using approach #2. Keep it under 200 words. Make the first sentence impossible to skip."

Now the AI has your context, your chosen strategy, and a clear constraint. The output will be dramatically better than a one-shot prompt.

**Layer 4 — Refine:**
> "Make it more conversational. The third paragraph feels stiff. And suggest a better subject line — the current one is generic."

Now you're editing, not starting over.

**Why this works:**

Each layer gives the AI more to work with. By the time you hit Layer 3, it's not guessing anymore — it's working with real constraints and a clear direction. The result is something that actually sounds like you and does what you need.

**This is how I build everything at V-Real AI.** The scripts, the strategy documents, the product pages — none of them come from a single prompt. They come from 4-8 layers of conversation where each step refines the last.

**Try it today:**

Pick something you need to write this week. Instead of asking AI to write it in one shot, try the layer cake:

1. Context first (who you are, who it's for, what you need)
2. Strategy next (outline approaches, pick one)
3. Draft (with clear constraints)
4. Refine (fix what's off, tighten what's loose)

You'll be surprised how much better the output is.
