# V-Real AI: MrBeast DNA Playbook

## How to think like MrBeast — adapted for documentary content

This document codifies MrBeast's proven YouTube frameworks and applies them
specifically to V-Real AI's documentary format. Every video MUST follow these
rules before publishing.

### The Foundation

MrBeast spent 20,000-30,000 hours studying YouTube. His words:
> "There's a five-year point in my life where I was just relentlessly,
> unhealthily obsessed with studying virality, studying the YouTube algorithm."

His 36-page leaked production handbook is given to every new employee on

## 1. THE 1/10 RULE — 90% of success is decided before you record

MrBeast's core philosophy: the concept, title, and thumbnail determine
whether a video succeeds or fails. The actual video is the last 10%.

### For V-Real AI:
- **Before writing a single word of script**, the title and thumbnail
  concept must be locked and validated
- Title + thumbnail must create a "curiosity gap" — the viewer MUST
  click to resolve the tension
- If you can't make a compelling title + thumbnail, the topic is wrong.
  Kill it and pick a different angle

---

## 2. THE FIRST 5 SECONDS — You have 5 seconds or you're dead

MrBeast's leaked production doc: "The first minute of every video we
ever make should be the most intriguing, awe-inspiring minute you can
make. You want them saying WOW every second."

### From the leaked document:
> "The first minute of the video is the most important minute of each video"
> — because that is when the highest percentage of viewers click away.

**Specific rules from the doc:**
- **Validate the thumbnail immediately.** If your thumbnail shows a
  woman's shocked face at 10 AM, the video must start with that moment.
  "Your title and thumbnail set expectations. At the very beginning,
  assure them that those expectations are being met."
- **Tell the viewer what they gain within the first two sentences.**
- **Show, don't tell.** MrBeast calls this "crazy progression" — cover
  massive ground in the first minute. Don't slowly build up.
- **"Jenga Storytelling"** — show a quick peek of the end result at the
  beginning to keep viewers motivated to watch.
- MrBeast maintains ~90% retention in the first 30 seconds.

### The Hook Formula (adapted for documentaries):
1. **Second 0-2**: Hit with the STAKES. Not the topic. The stakes.
   - BAD: "Today we're going to talk about AI changing marketing"
   - GOOD: "She lost her entire team in 10 minutes. Then she tripled her salary."

2. **Second 2-5**: Create the CURIOSITY GAP
   - Tease the outcome but don't reveal how
   - "The difference between her and the 3.2 million people who didn't make it? A single decision in the first 90 days."

3. **Second 5-15**: Establish CREDIBILITY + STAKES
   - "I tracked 47 people across 8 industries through this exact transformation."
   - Show the data. Show you did the work.

4. **Second 15-30**: The PROMISE
   - Tell them exactly what they'll get if they keep watching
   - "By the end of this video, you'll know the three actions that separate survivors from casualties."

### EP001 Hook Rewrite (MrBeast DNA):
OLD: "Sarah Chen's phone buzzed at nine forty-seven AM..."
NEW: "Half her team was gone by 10 AM. Replaced by AI tools that cost $49 a month. But here's what nobody expected — the people who moved fast saw 28% salary increases. The people who froze? Eight months to recover. I tracked 47 people through this exact moment. Three patterns emerged. And the difference came down to one decision in the first 90 days."

Why it's better: Stakes in the first sentence. Specific numbers create credibility. Curiosity gap ("one decision"). Promise of framework ("three patterns").

## 3. EVERY SECOND MUST EARN THE NEXT SECOND

MrBeast's cardinal rule: "There should be no dull moments. Every second
of the video should be interesting enough to keep the viewer watching."

From the leaked doc: If a 10-minute video only has 6 minutes of content,
make it a 6-minute video. Don't pad. Ever.

### For V-Real AI:
- **Cut ruthlessly**. If a sentence doesn't advance the story or add
  emotional weight, delete it
- **No throat-clearing**. No "So basically what happened was..." or
  "Let me explain why this matters..."
- **Front-load value**. Don't save the best insight for the end.
  Give incredible value at minute 1, then keep escalating
- **The "would I click away?" test**: At every 30-second mark, ask
  "If I were a viewer, would I still be watching?" If the answer is
  maybe — rewrite that section

## 4. RE-HOOKS — Fighting the mid-video dip

MrBeast re-hooks viewers every 2-3 minutes with a new promise,
a new tension, or a new character. The viewer should feel like
they're watching a new video every few minutes.

### V-Real AI Re-Hook Schedule:
- **0:00** — Main hook (stakes + curiosity gap)
- **1:30** — Character hook ("Meet Sarah Chen. Three days after the announcement...")
- **3:15** — Contrast hook ("Mike Rodriguez made a different choice...")
- **4:45** — Redemption hook ("David Park saw what others missed...")
- **6:00** — Framework reveal ("This is the 90-Day Rule")
- **7:30** — Direct challenge ("If you're watching this today, here's what you do")
- **8:30** — Future tease ("The next episode drops Tuesday")

Each re-hook should feel like a mini-hook that could stand alone.

## 5. TITLE RULES

From MrBeast's strategies:
- **Under 50 characters** — shorter titles get higher CTR
- **Front-load the hook word** — the most intriguing word goes first
- **Use numbers when possible** — "28%" is more clickable than "significant"
- **Create a curiosity gap** — the title makes you NEED to click
- **Never clickbait** — the video MUST deliver on the title's promise
- **Avoid generic words** — "Amazing", "Incredible", "Must Watch" are noise
- **Test 20+ title variations** before picking one

## 6. THUMBNAIL RULES

The formula: **One Face + One Object + One Question.** If you have to
squint to understand it, it has failed.

From MrBeast's A/B testing and leaked doc:
- **3 elements max** — too much visual information kills CTR
- **One clear focal point** — the eye should go to ONE thing
- **Colors: Blue, Red, Green, Yellow** — saturated to 100%. Never muted.
  Super-bright saturated colors pop out of the YouTube feed.
- **Faces with GENUINE EMOTION** — not posed. Excitement, surprise, shock.
  Humans are naturally drawn to strong emotional facial expressions.
- **Text: 1-2 words max** — MrBeast does NOT rely heavily on words
- **The "Zoom Out" test** — if the thumbnail is not readable at 10% size,
  delete elements until it is. Always zoom to 10% before publishing.
- **Closed mouth > open mouth** — A/B tests showed closed mouth gets
  longer watch times
- **Must tell a different story than the title** — title + thumbnail
  create a 1+1=3 effect, not repeat each other

**Testing methodology (exact process):**
- Generate ~50 thumbnail/title concepts per video
- Create 10-20 thumbnail variations for every single video
- Test with small groups before going live
- After publishing, actively swap thumbnails based on performance data
- Thumbnails designed BEFORE the video is filmed — if a compelling
  thumbnail can't be designed, the video idea gets scrapped

### EP001 Thumbnail Brief (MrBeast DNA):
- Dark background (#0A0F1E)
- Split composition: Left side shows a woman at a desk looking confident
  (lit in cyan #00D4FF). Right side shows an empty desk/chair (dark, ominous)
- Text: "90 DAYS" in large bold
- No more than 3 visual elements total
- Must be readable at 100px height (phone search results)

## 7. RETENTION EDITING

MrBeast pioneered "retention editing" — visual techniques that prevent
the viewer's attention from drifting:

### Techniques (adapted for documentary):
1. **Cut every 3-5 seconds** — no static shot longer than 5 seconds
2. **Zoom punch** — subtle 5-10% zoom to emphasize key moments
3. **B-roll variety** — never use the same visual style for more than 15 seconds
4. **Data cards** — full-screen stat callouts break up the visual monotony
5. **Lower thirds** — character introductions add production value
6. **Sound design** — subtle whooshes on transitions, bass hits on reveals
7. **Pattern interrupts** — every 15-20 seconds, SOMETHING changes visually
8. **Color temperature shifts** — warm scenes for positive stories, cool for negative
9. **Speed ramps** — slight slow-mo on emotional moments

### CRITICAL — MrBeast's Evolution (2024-2025):
MrBeast tweeted: "This past year I've slowed down our videos, focused on
story telling, let scenes breathe, yelled less, more personality, longer
videos, etc." Cut count dropped from 38 cuts/minute to ~23 cuts/minute.

He confirmed in Sept 2025: "Yup, this video is a good representation of
new MrBeast content. Less screaming, slower, more story, emotion, etc."

His camera lead said: "Emotion and, beyond that, the through line of the
story is going to be the biggest thing."

**For V-Real AI:** This is PERFECT for us. The new MrBeast direction IS
documentary storytelling. We use selective retention editing — visual
variety serves the story, not replaces it. Let emotional moments breathe.
Use cuts and zooms to EMPHASIZE, not to overstimulate.

### The Minute-by-Minute Blueprint (from leaked doc):
- **0:00-1:00**: Most important minute. Validate thumbnail. Front-load
  information. Maximum visuals, music, effects, quick scene changes.
- **1:00-3:00**: Transition into the story. Use "crazy progression" to
  cover ground fast and build emotional investment.
- **3:00**: RE-ENGAGEMENT SPECTACLE. Something impressive. This is where
  viewers typically get bored and click off if you don't intervene.
- **3:00-6:00**: "Most exciting and interesting content that is also very
  simple." Quick scene changes, highly stimulating content. Make viewers
  fall in love with the story.
- **6:00**: Another re-engagement point for the second half.
- **Midpoint**: Throw in a twist — surprise, unexpected turn, unforeseen
  obstacle. Ramps tension when viewers think they know what's coming.
- **Ending**: Satisfying payoff + ABRUPT ending. Keep energy high until
  the very last moment. Do NOT let the video taper off.

## 8. THE THREE METRICS THAT MATTER

MrBeast's simplest explanation of the algorithm:
> "If people are clicking your video more than they click other videos
> and they're watching it longer than they watch other videos, as
> simplistic as that is, that's what YouTube wants."

His mindset from VidCon 2022: Replace "How do I satisfy the algorithm?"
with "How do I satisfy the audience?" The algorithm reflects human
psychology — there is no video the algorithm dislikes; it is the viewers
who may not like it.

MrBeast focuses on exactly three YouTube metrics:

1. **CTR (Click-Through Rate)** — "Are people clicking?"
   - Target: 8-15% for new channels
   - Driven by: Title + Thumbnail
   - If CTR is low, the video concept/packaging is wrong

2. **AVD (Average View Duration)** — "Are people watching?"
   - Target: 50%+ of video length
   - Driven by: Hook + Content quality + Editing
   - If AVD is low, the video has dull moments

3. **Audience Retention Graph** — "Where are people leaving?"
   - Watch for: drops in the first 30 seconds (bad hook),
     drops at mid-video (no re-hooks), gradual decline (padding)
   - Every dip is a lesson for the next video

### V-Real AI Targets:
- CTR: >10% (achievable with strong title + thumbnail)
- AVD: >60% (9-minute video = viewers watch 5.4+ minutes)
- First 30s retention: >85% (if below this, the hook failed)

## 13. NO DULL MOMENTS + ABRUPT ENDINGS

> "Hook people at the start of the video, transition them to an amazing
> story that they are invested in, have no dull moments, and then have
> a satisfying payoff at the end of the video with an abrupt ending."

> "The video endings must always be abrupt to protect retention."

> "Don't ever signal the end of the video unless it's to build hype
> for the prize or payoff at the end."

### For V-Real AI:
- The brand close ("Subscribe for insights...") must feel like a PUNCH,
  not a wind-down. Cut to end screen immediately after the final
  powerful line. No fade. No pause. Abrupt.
- Never say "So in conclusion..." or "To wrap up..." — those are
  signals that tell the viewer to leave
- The last spoken line should be the most memorable. Not a CTA.
  The CTA is the pinned comment. The last line is the feeling.

EP001 last line: "The question is: which side of it will you choose
to be on?" — then HARD CUT to end screen. No "thanks for watching."
