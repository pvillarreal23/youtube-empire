## THE RULE

No script goes to record until it scores 10/10 across all models and all agents.

## THE PROCESS

### STEP 1 — FIRST DRAFT
Write the initial script. Target ~1,200 words / 8–9 min.
Voice: BBC/Netflix documentary. Cinematic. Rhythmic. NOT hustle content.

### STEP 2 — 6-AGENT INTERNAL REVIEW
Run the full internal crew on the draft. Each agent reviews from their specific lens:

- **Marcus (Exec Producer)** — Is it post-ready? Does the open earn the audience in 10 seconds? Does the close land clean on the brand?
- **Zara (Creative Director)** — Is the voice consistent throughout? Does it breathe for documentary narration? Any register breaks?
- **Leo (Audience Strategist)** — YouTube retention. Where does the viewer almost drop? Is the EP002 subscribe hook in the right place?
- **Dana (Script Doctor)** — Line-level polish only. Weak verbs, redundancy, momentum-killers. Exact replacements.
- **GPT "The Challenger"** — Devil's advocate. What claims can a skeptic attack? What feels unearned?
- **Gem "The Researcher"** — Factual grounding. Are all claims defensible? Are specific numbers accurate?

Apply agreed changes. Hold your ground where the voice requires it.

### STEP 3 — GEMINI PRESSURE TEST
Send full script to Gemini. Ask for:
- Overall rating (X/10)
- Specific lines that weaken it
- Suggested replacements

Cherry-pick only. Do NOT wholesale replace with Gemini's rewrite.

### STEP 4 — CHATGPT PRESSURE TEST
Send full script to ChatGPT. Ask for:
- Overall rating (X/10)
- What would make it a 10
- Suggested line-level improvements

Cherry-pick only. Do NOT adopt ChatGPT's structure or cadence — it fragments into hustle content.

### STEP 5 — GROK PRESSURE TEST
Send full script to Grok. Ask for:
- Overall rating (X/10)
- Specific rewrites to strengthen

Cherry-pick only. Watch for clichés ("exponential world", "lead or be invisible").

### STEP 6 — CHERRY-PICK SYNTHESIS
Review all model feedback. For each suggestion, ask:
1. Does this line say something more *precisely* than what we had?
2. Does it stay in the V-Real AI voice?
3. Does it *earn* its specificity through the surrounding narrative?

If yes to all three → take it.
If it's a wholesale structural change → reject it.

### STEP 7 — MERGE + FINAL 6-AGENT PASS
Apply all cherry-picked lines. Run one final 6-agent pass on the merged version.
Overrule any team calls that conflict with the channel's core voice principles (see below).

### STEP 8 — RATE + LOCK
Get 10/10 consensus. If not there, identify exactly what's missing and fix it.
Save as `ep[XXX]-script-vFINAL.md` in Downloads.
Record.

## V-REAL AI VOICE PRINCIPLES
(Non-negotiable. These override all model suggestions.)

**Rhythm** — Short lines. Strategic pauses. Sentences that breathe.
Every line should be readable aloud in one breath or structured to land in silence.

**Documentary, not hustle** — No bullet points in narration. No section headers.
No "In an exponential world..." No "Lead or be invisible."
Think BBC/Netflix. Not Gary Vee.

**Concrete beats abstract** — Specific numbers, specific verbs, specific claims.
"$50-a-month stack" > "basic subscription stack"
"2 hours" > "very quickly"

**Earned specificity** — A specific claim only works if the narrative built to it.
The $50 number earns its place because the founder story set the scale.
Don't drop specifics cold.

**Intimacy over declaration** — The closer should meet the viewer where they are,
not declare something at them.
"You're not paranoid. You're observant." > "Welcome to the Era of the Architect."
"This is V-Real AI." > "And this is where it starts."

**Identity, not motivation** — Never tell the viewer what to do.
Name what they already feel. Let them draw the conclusion.

## WHAT TO CHERRY-PICK VS. REJECT

### TAKE IT IF:
- It says something more precisely than what you had
- It's a single line or two-line beat, not a structural change
- It fits the documentary cadence when read aloud
- It earns its claim through the surrounding narrative

### REJECT IT IF:
- It's a wholesale structural rewrite
- It introduces bullet points or section headers
- It sounds like a TED talk, a LinkedIn post, or a motivational reel
- It breaks the rhythm when read aloud
- It swaps concrete specifics for vague abstractions (or vice versa without earning it)
