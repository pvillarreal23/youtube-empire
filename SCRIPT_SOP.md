# V-Real AI — Script Standard Operating Procedure

## The Rule

No script ships until it scores 10/10 across all models and agents. This is not a guideline. This is the standard.

---

## MrBeast Gate (Pre-Script)

Before writing a single word, these must be LOCKED:

1. **Title** — 20+ variations tested, strongest selected
2. **Thumbnail concept** — Would you click this at mobile thumbnail size?
3. **Hook sentence** — The first words out of Julian's mouth
4. **Re-hook schedule** — Planned pattern interrupts at 2:00, 4:00, 6:00, 8:00
5. **"Would I click away?" audit** — Review every section transition for retention risk

If any of these feel weak, stop. Fix before writing.

---

## Step 0 — Concept Validation

- Generate 20+ title variations
- Test each against: Would I click? Does it create a curiosity gap? Is it specific enough?
- Concept must make the team say "I'd click that"
- Lock title + thumbnail concept before proceeding

---

## Step 1 — First Draft

**Owner:** Scriptwriter
**Target:** ~1,200 words / 8-9 minutes
**Voice:** BBC/Netflix documentary — intimate, concrete, never preachy

### Hook Structure (First 30 Seconds)
- **0-2s:** Stakes — why should I care RIGHT NOW?
- **2-5s:** Curiosity gap — information gap the viewer must close
- **5-15s:** Credibility — brief proof point
- **15-30s:** Promise — exactly what they'll gain by watching

### Body Structure
- 3-5 clear sections with mini-hooks between each
- Pattern interrupts every 60-90 seconds
- Open loops teasing upcoming sections
- Proof points for every claim (data, examples, stories)
- Objection handling before viewer skepticism

### Closing (Final 60 Seconds)
- Payoff — deliver on opening promise
- Single, clear CTA
- Emotional close — memorable note

---

## Step 3 — Gemini Pressure Test

Submit script to Gemini with:
> "Rate this script on a scale of 1-10 for a BBC/Netflix-style AI documentary YouTube video. Provide specific line suggestions for improvement. Be harsh."

Take only lines that are **more precise** and **fit the voice**.

---

## Step 4 — ChatGPT Pressure Test

Submit script to ChatGPT with:
> "Rate this script on a scale of 1-10 for a faceless YouTube documentary about AI. Focus on retention, emotional impact, and specificity. Suggest improvements line by line."

Take only what's **stronger**. Reject wholesale rewrites.

---

## Step 5 — Grok Pressure Test

Submit script to Grok with:
> "Rate this script for a documentary YouTube channel. Be critical. What would you rewrite? What's weak? Score 1-10."

Take only what **sharpens the edge**.

---

## Step 6 — Cherry-Pick Synthesis

Review all model feedback. Apply these rules:

### TAKE
- Single lines or two-line beats that are more precise
- Specific data points that strengthen claims
- Better verbs, tighter phrasing
- Stronger analogies or metaphors

### REJECT
- Wholesale rewrites (these break the voice)
- Bullet points (not documentary style)
- TED Talk tone (too performative)
- Rhythm breaks (the cadence is deliberate)
- Hustle language ("exponential," "game-changing," "revolutionize")
- Anything that sounds like a blog post

---

## Step 7 — Merge + Final 6-Agent Pass

1. Merge cherry-picked improvements into the script
2. Run one final 6-agent review on the merged version
3. Each agent confirms: is this better or worse than before?
4. Any regression = revert that change

---

## Step 8 — Rate + Lock

- All 6 agents must rate 10/10
- All pressure test models must rate 9+/10
- Lock the script: `ep[XXX]-script-vFINAL.md`
- No further changes allowed after lock (except factual corrections)

## V-Real AI Voice Principles (Non-Negotiable)

These define what V-Real AI sounds like. Every line must pass:

### 1. Rhythm
Short lines. Strategic pauses. One breath or silence between beats. The script has a pulse.

### 2. Documentary, Not Hustle
BBC/Netflix tone. No bullet points. No "exponential world." No "let's dive in." No "game-changing." We observe. We reveal. We let the viewer feel it.

### 3. Concrete Beats Abstract
"147% increase" beats "massive growth." "3.2 million people" beats "millions." Specific numbers, specific verbs, specific claims.

### 4. Earned Specificity
A specific claim only works if the narrative has been built to support it. Don't drop stats without context. Earn the moment.

### 5. Intimacy Over Declaration
"You've felt it too" beats "The world is changing." Meet the viewer where they are. Speak to one person, not an audience.

### 6. Identity, Not Motivation
Name what they feel. Never tell them what to do. "You're not paranoid. You're observant." Not "You should pay attention to AI."

## Benchmark Lines (EP001 "The Shift")

These are the quality floor. Every future script must match this standard:

> "No one sent an announcement. No one rang a bell. The rules just changed."

> "They no longer require mastery of execution. They require taste."

> "The barrier to entry didn't just lower. It evaporated."

> "You're not paranoid. You're observant."
