### 2.2 Video Format Recommendations

**PRIMARY FORMAT: Long-form Documentary (8-12 minutes)**

**Structure (Scriptwriter Standard):**
- **Hook (0:00-0:30):** 3-second stop-scroll moment + strong opinion
- **Act 1 - Setup (0:30-2:00):** Context, stakes, "why you should care"
- **Act 2 - Core Content (2:00-9:00):** Value delivery with visual cues, b-roll, screen recordings, breakdowns
- **Act 3 - Payoff (9:00-11:00):** Resolution, key takeaway, transformation
- **CTA (11:00-12:00):** Clear next action (subscribe, newsletter, community post)

**Visual Style (Video Editor + Thumbnail Designer):**
- **B-roll:** Screen recordings (tool interfaces), AI-generated graphics (Midjourney), real footage of AI in use
- **Pacing:** Cut every 3-5 seconds, shorter for fast-paced content, longer for explanatory moments (never 10+ seconds static)
- **Text Overlays:** Key terms, numbers, tool names (2-3 words max per overlay, high contrast)
- **Transitions:** Subtle cuts preferred over flashy transitions (authenticity over overproduction)
- **Color Grading:** Consistent temperature across videos, slightly blue-tinted for tech authority

**SECONDARY FORMAT: YouTube Shorts & Short-form Clips (60-90 seconds)**

**Strategy (Shorts & Clips Agent):**
- Extract 3-5 clip-worthy moments from each long-form video (automation via Make.com)
- Platform-native adaptation: TikTok hook differs from YouTube Shorts differs from Instagram Reels
- Maximum production lift: Use auto-captions from long-form, minimal re-editing
- Cross-promotion: Shorts link to full video, full video embeds highest-performing short

**Posting Cadence by Format:**

| Format | Frequency | Why |
|--------|-----------|-----|
| Long-form (8-12 min) | 2-3 per week | Sustainable production pace, 70% of channel authority |
| YouTube Shorts | Daily (derived from long-form) | Algorithm boost, viewer discovery, path to channel |
| Instagram Reels | 3x per week | Business audience, traffic back to YouTube |
| TikTok | 4x per week | Trending audio, viral amplification, younger audience |
| Twitter/X Threads | 2x per week | Industry professionals, source links back to YouTube |
| LinkedIn | 1x per week | Career/professional positioning |
| Email Newsletter | 1x per week | Revenue + community retention |

### 2.5 Hook Strategy for First 30 Seconds

**Golden Rule (Hook Specialist):** Every hook must do THREE things:
1. **Stop the scroll** (3-second visual + audio hook)
2. **Create a knowledge gap** (what will you learn that you don't know?)
3. **Promise immediate value** (why should YOU care today?)

**Hook Formula Variations (tested across tier 4 agents):**

**FORMULA 1: Specific Number/Claim**
- "I replaced my entire marketing team with 6 Claude agents"
- "This AI tool costs $19/month and does what costs $500K in salary"
- "Claude 3.5 is 47% cheaper and 30% faster than GPT-4o"
- Pattern: [Specific number] + [unexpected claim] + [immediate implication]

**FORMULA 2: Contrarian Take**
- "Everyone's teaching you AI wrong"
- "The tool everyone recommends is a scam"
- "Your favorite AI tool is about to become obsolete"
- Pattern: [Audience assumption] + [contradiction] + [proof coming]

**FORMULA 3: Narrative Mystery**
- "I found something while testing Claude that OpenAI doesn't want you to know"
- "After testing 47 AI tools, one completely changed my business"
- "Here's what happened when I automated my entire job"
- Pattern: [Personal action] + [surprising outcome] + [explanation coming]

**FORMULA 4: Direct Benefit/Cost**
- "Save 10 hours a week with this automation workflow"
- "Here's how to make $5K/month without selling anything"
- "Stop wasting money on this tool — this one is free and better"
- Pattern: [Specific benefit] + [timeframe] + [tool/method]

**FORMULA 5: Industry Moment**
- "AI just hit an inflection point and nobody noticed"
- "This acquisition signals the end of AI startups as we know them"
- "Regulation just changed the entire AI business model"
- Pattern: [Industry event] + [implications] + [what you need to do]

**ANTI-PATTERNS to Avoid (Storyteller + Hook Specialist consensus):**
- "So in today's video..." (generic, kills momentum)
- "Let's dive into..." (overused, no specificity)
- "This is insane/crazy/wild" (lazy adjectives, no substance)
- "You won't believe..." (clickbait, viewer resentment)
- "I'm going to show you..." (passive, no commitment)

**Hook Delivery (Voice Director settings):**
- Speed: Slightly faster than normal (1.1-1.2x perceived pace)
- Energy: High confidence, slight surprise inflection
- Pauses: Minimal in hook, strategic pause before revelation
- Emphasis: Heavy stress on the specific number/claim

### 3.2 Voiceover Approach

**Standard Setting (Voice Director):**
- **Voice:** Aaron (ElevenLabs premium voice)
- **Model:** eleven_multilingual_v2
- **Stability:** 0.71 (natural variation, not robotic)
- **Similarity:** 0.75 (authentic to original voice)
- **Style:** 0.45 (conversational tone, not theatrical)
- **Speaker Boost:** ON (clarity and presence)

**Voiceover Process:**
1. **Script Markup:** Scriptwriter flags emotional beats, pause points, emphasis words
2. **Pacing Map:** Voice Director adds specific notes for each section (speed, tone, emotion)
3. **Generation:** Create 2-3 variations with different settings
4. **Listening Review:** Voice Director + Scriptwriter listen for authenticity (no robotic delivery)
5. **Adjustment:** Modify stability/similarity/style based on performance
6. **Final Approval:** Voice Director signs off on final cut

**Tone Variance by Section (Voice Director Guidance):**
- **Hook:** High energy, confident, slight surprise inflection
- **Setup:** Conversational, relaxed, genuine curiosity
- **Main Content:** Educational but dynamic, emphasis on key points
- **Payoff:** Slightly more reflective, thoughtful pacing
- **CTA:** Warm, encouraging, slight downstep in pace

**What Makes VoiceOver Sound Human (Storyteller checklist):**
- ✓ Pacing varies (not consistently fast or slow)
- ✓ Breathing audible (slight breath between major points)
- ✓ Thinking pauses present ("and that's... actually kind of important")
- ✓ Self-correction moments ("wait, I said that wrong earlier")
- ✓ Emphasis lands on conversational stress points, not random words
- ✓ Inflection rises/falls naturally (questions vs. statements)

### 3.3 Visual Style Guidelines

**Thumbnail Aesthetic (Thumbnail Designer):**

**Core Formula:**
- **Background:** Bold, high-contrast color (red, orange, blue, green) OR dark background for premium feel
- **Subject:** 1 clear focal point (tool logo, person's face with expression, number, or visual metaphor)
- **Text:** 2-4 words MAXIMUM, high contrast, readable at 160x90px mobile size
- **Composition:** Rule of thirds, focal point off-center
- **Typography:** Bold sans-serif (Impact or similar), white/black on colored background
- **Emotion Target:** What should the viewer FEEL? (Curiosity, FOMO, authority, surprise)

**Thumbnail Text Rules:**
- Never repeat the title verbatim
- Never use lowercase (impact of uppercase proven by A/B test data)
- Never exceed 4 words
- Provocative adjectives preferred (FREE, NEW, HIDDEN, RANKING, EXPOSED)
- Avoid generic terms (MUST SEE, IMPORTANT, WATCH)

**Color Palette Rotation (Branding):**
- Video 1-3: Orange/red (energy, attention)
- Video 4-5: Blue (authority, trust, tech)
- Video 6-7: Green (money, growth, positive)
- Video 8-9: Purple (innovation, insight)
- Video 10: Red/orange (cycle restart)

**B-Roll Standards (Video Editor):**
- Screen recordings: Cursor visible, intentional pace (not rushed), 1080p minimum
- AI-generated graphics: Consistent art style (Midjourney style preset maintained)
- Real-world footage: Stable camera, good lighting, relevant to narration
- Charts/data: Clean design, readable labels, animated entrance when possible

**Lower Thirds & Overlays:**
- Format: Agent name, title, company (when applicable)
- Duration: 3-5 seconds minimum
- Font: Consistent with thumbnail aesthetic

**Opening/Closing Sequence:**
- Duration: 2 seconds opening, 3-5 seconds closing

### 3.4 Thumbnail Formula

**STEP-BY-STEP DESIGN PROCESS (Thumbnail Designer Workflow):**

**1. Extract Visual Moment from Script**
- Identify the most striking image/moment from the video topic
- Example: "Claude is faster" → stopwatch visual, "AI replaces X" → visual of replacement, "This tool costs $19" → large "$19" text

**2. Define Emotion Target**
- Hook: What should viewers FEEL when they see this?
- Options: Curiosity (?) | FOMO (missing out) | Authority (credible) | Surprise (contradiction) | Greed (money opportunity)

**3. Select Color from Rotation**
- Use palette rotation system above
- Test contrast: light text on dark background, or dark text on light background
- Midjourney will be primary generation tool

**4. Compose Text (2-4 Words)**
- Avoid: Descriptive ("AI Tools"), Generic ("MUST WATCH"), Duplicative of title
- Ideal: Provocative ("FREE"), Specific ("RANKED"), Comparative ("VS")
- Examples: "FREE RANKING," "EXPOSED," "$10K/MO," "BEATS ChatGPT"

**5. Design in Figma or Midjourney**
- Midjourney prompt structure: "[emotion] [color] background, [subject], [text overlay], YouTube thumbnail style"
- Example: "Curiosity-driven orange background, stack of AI tools competing, text 'RANKED,' professional thumbnail style, 1280x720"

**6. Test at Mobile Size**
- View at 160x90px
- Verify text readable without zooming
- Verify focal point clear even at tiny size
- Verify color contrast sufficient for colorblind viewers

**7. A/B Test 2 Versions**
- Title version: Emphasize headline text
- Visual version: Emphasize striking image
- Launch with visual version (higher CTR based on data)
- Test alternative after 50 views
