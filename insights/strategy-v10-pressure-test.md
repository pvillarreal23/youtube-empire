3. **Data-First Authority:** Every claim backed by tool name, version, date, source. Proprietary monthly AI tool benchmarks (original data others cite).

4. **Community Voice:** Featured comments, viewer challenges, audience success stories.

**Competitive Moat:**
- Niche specificity (AI for marketing, not general AI)
- Narrative documentary format (unique in AI niche — competitors lecture, we tell stories)
- Verification standard (every tool claim tested, every number sourced)
- Proprietary benchmark data (original, citeable content)

## 3. CONTENT STRATEGY

### 3.1 Content Mix — Evergreen-First

| Format | % | Protagonist Needed? | Example |
|--------|---|---------------------|---------|
| **Breakdowns** (tool/trend analysis) | 45% | No | "Claude vs GPT-4o vs Gemini: The Real Benchmark" |
| **Playbooks** (tactical how-to) | 40% | No | "The $50/Month AI Stack That Replaced a $5K Team" |
| **Stories** (transformation narratives) | 15% | Yes | "She Lost Her Career — Then Tripled Her Income" |

**85% evergreen content. No protagonist dependency.** Stories are high-impact bonus content when sourced, not load-bearing infrastructure. At 3 videos/week (156/year), only ~23 need protagonists — achievable from community submissions alone once audience reaches 10K+ subs.

### 3.2 Story Sourcing (for 15% story content)

**Target: 2-3 stories/month (light requirement).**
- **Community form** in every description + pinned post (scales with audience)
- **SaaS partnerships** (Make.com, Jasper, Copy.ai surface customer stories for exposure)
- **Public case studies** (company blogs, conference talks — no consent needed for public info)
- **LinkedIn outreach** when needed (2-3 DMs/week)

**If sourcing dries up:** Shift that week to breakdown/playbook. Zero production disruption.

### 3.3 Video Structure — Phased Length Strategy

**Discovery Phase (Months 1-2): 4-6 minute videos**
- Purpose: Maximize completion rate → build algorithm trust for new channel
- Structure: Hook (15 sec) → Setup (30 sec) → Core (3-4 min) → CTA (30 sec)
- **Target completion rate: 35-45%** (realistic for new faceless educational channel; industry new-channel average is 25-35%, we aim above average due to cinematic quality)
- Critical: If average completion stays below 30% after 8 videos, reduce to 3-4 minute format and diagnose retention drop-off points.

**Growth Phase (Month 3+): 8-12 minute videos**
- **Transition trigger:** Completion rate >35% consistently across 10+ videos
- Structure: Hook (30 sec) → Act 1 (90 sec) → Act 2 (6-7 min) → Act 3 (2 min) → CTA (1 min)

### 3.4 Faceless Hook Strategy (First 15 Seconds)

- **Second 0-3:** Bold visual (cinematic B-roll, data viz, text animation) + Julian mid-sentence (no preamble)
- **Second 3-8:** Knowledge gap claim ("A designer lost her career in 20 minutes. Then she made 3x more.")
- **Second 8-15:** Stakes + promise ("What she figured out is what most people haven't. By the end, you will.")
- **Visual rhythm:** New visual every 2-3 seconds. No static shots. Movement = retention.

### 3.5 YouTube Shorts (Day 1)

- 3-5 clips from each long-form + 2 original Shorts/week
- Daily output target
- Each ends: "Full story on the channel"

**Phase 3 — Months 9+ (at $5K+ revenue):**
- Phase 2 + freelance editor ($500-1K) + keyword tools ($50-100) + thumbnail designer ($200-400)
- **Total: $1,000-$2,025/mo**

### Production Stress Scenarios

| Scenario | Trigger | Response |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Quality demands exceed budget | Phase 2 needs $1K/mo but revenue is $600 | Reduce to 2 videos/week, invest savings in quality over quantity |
| API costs spike 50-100% | Model price increases | Downgrade to smaller/cheaper models for research tasks, keep premium for scripts/QA only |
| Freelance editor unavailable | Can't find reliable editor | Use AI-assisted editing (Remotion templates) + invest extra time in self-QA |
| Revenue stalls at $2K/mo | Month 8+ with no growth | Cut production costs to Phase 1 levels ($120-295), focus 100% on content quality over volume |

**Guiding principle:** Never spend more than 30% of trailing-3-month average revenue on production. If revenue is $0, production cost ceiling is $300/mo (Phase 1 budget).

### 8.2 AI Agent Pipeline

**Phase 1 (6 core agents):** Researcher → Scriptwriter → Voice Director → Editor → SEO Specialist → Quality Lead

**Activation:** Month 2 (+3 agents), Month 4 (+2), Month 6+ (full council as volume demands)

**Quality Gate:** Multi-model pressure test. 9+/10 from all reviewers or revision loop (max 5 cycles).

### 8.3 Audience Feedback Loop

- Weekly comment analysis → future video topics
- Monthly community polls
- Scene-by-scene retention analysis per video (identify drop-off points)
- A/B title/thumbnail swap at 48h if below target
- **Monthly strategy review:** Actuals vs. projections. If off by >40% in any metric for 2 consecutive months, trigger strategy pivot.

### 8.4 Pivot Triggers

| Metric | Threshold | Action |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Completion rate <25% for 5+ videos | Critical | Reduce video length, overhaul hook strategy, test new format |
| CTR <2.5% for 5+ videos | Critical | Overhaul thumbnails + titles, test new content pillars |
| Subscriber growth <500/mo by Month 4 | Warning | Analyze top-performing video, double down on that format |
| Revenue <$1K/mo by Month 8 | Warning | Cut costs to Phase 1, focus on audience growth over monetization |
| All formats underperform | Critical | Consider niche pivot (broaden to all professionals, or narrow to specific industry) |

### 8.5 Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Algorithm change | Newsletter (owned audience), SEO (search traffic), Shorts diversification |
| AI content saturation | Niche focus + narrative format + verification standard as moats |
| Voice detection stigma | Premium voice, transparent about AI-assisted production |
| Faceless growth ceiling | Guest experts, community voice, "Julian's desk" visual identity |
| API cost inflation | 50-100% buffer; can downgrade models for non-critical tasks |

1. **YouTube-first.** One platform. Master it.
