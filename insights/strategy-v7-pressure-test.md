## 3. CONTENT STRATEGY

### 3.1 Content Mix (3 Formats)

| Format | % | Example |
|--------|---|---------|
| **Stories** (protagonist-driven narratives) | 50% | "She Lost Her Design Career in 20 Minutes — Then Tripled Her Income" |
| **Breakdowns** (tool/trend deep-dives) | 30% | "Claude vs GPT-4o vs Gemini: The Real Benchmark Nobody's Showing You" |
| **Playbooks** (tactical how-to) | 20% | "The $50/Month AI Stack That Replaced a $5,000 Team" |

### 3.2 Protagonist Sourcing System

Every story episode needs a real person. Here's the sourcing pipeline:

1. **Reddit/Twitter Mining:** Monitor r/ChatGPT, r/artificial, r/freelance, AI Twitter for "AI changed my career" stories (automated via Make.com webhook)
2. **Audience Submissions:** "Share your AI story" form linked in every video description + pinned community post
3. **LinkedIn Outreach:** Direct message professionals posting about AI career transitions (2-3 per week)
4. **Verification:** Every protagonist story requires: (a) real name or credible alias, (b) verifiable details (company type, timeframe, tool used), (c) consent for anonymized storytelling
5. **Story Bank:** Maintain rolling backlog of 10+ verified stories. Never publish without 4-week buffer.

**Liability Shield:** All stories use "let's call her [name]" framing with anonymized details. No claims that can't be verified. Revenue figures presented as ranges, not exact amounts.

### 3.3 Video Structure (8-12 minutes)

- **Hook (0:00-0:30):** 3-second stop-scroll + bold claim + knowledge gap
- **Act 1 — Setup (0:30-2:00):** Stakes, context, "why you should care TODAY"
- **Act 2 — Core (2:00-9:00):** Story/breakdown with visual cues, data, B-roll
- **Act 3 — Payoff (9:00-11:00):** Resolution, key takeaway, transformation
- **CTA (11:00-12:00):** Subscribe + specific next action

### 3.4 YouTube Shorts Strategy (From Day 1)

- Extract 3-5 clip-worthy moments from each long-form video
- Standalone Shorts: 2x/week original micro-stories (60-90 sec)
- Target: Daily Shorts output (mix of clips + originals)
- Purpose: Algorithm discovery → funnel to long-form → subscriber conversion
- Each Short ends with: "Full story on the channel"

## 6. SEO & DISCOVERY

### 6.1 Keyword Strategy

**Primary Keywords (Monthly Search Volume):**
- "AI tools" — 110K/mo (high competition, target via Shorts)
- "best AI tools 2026" — 22K/mo (medium competition, long-form target)
- "AI automation" — 18K/mo (medium, playbook content)
- "AI replacing jobs" — 14K/mo (low-medium, story content)
- "Claude vs ChatGPT" — 8K/mo (low competition, breakdown content)

**Long-Tail Strategy:**
- "[Tool name] tutorial 2026" — low competition, high purchase intent
- "AI workflow automation [industry]" — niche, high value
- "how to use [tool] for [task]" — beginner-friendly, high volume

### 6.2 Thumbnail Strategy

**Formula:** Bold color background + 1 focal point + 2-4 words max
- Readable at 160x90px (mobile-first)
- A/B test 2 versions per video (swap at 48 hours if CTR < 4%)
- Color rotation: Red → Blue → Green → Orange (prevent gallery monotony)
- Text never repeats title — it adds curiosity layer

### 6.3 Title Optimization

**Hook Formulas (rotate):**
1. Specific number + unexpected claim: "6 AI Agents Replaced My Marketing Team"
2. Contrarian take: "Everyone's Teaching AI Wrong"
3. Narrative mystery: "After Testing 47 AI Tools, One Changed Everything"
4. Direct benefit: "Save 10 Hours/Week With This Automation"

## 8. PRODUCTION SYSTEM

### 8.1 AI-Powered Pipeline (Lean Phase 1)

The production system uses AI agents organized in a pipeline. **Phase 1 runs with 6 core agents** (not all 33):

| Agent | Role | Tool |
|-------|------|------|
| Researcher | Topic discovery + fact-checking | Claude + web search |
| Scriptwriter | Script drafting + revision | Claude |
| Voice Director | Voiceover generation + QA | ElevenLabs |
| Editor | Video assembly + B-roll sourcing | Pexels + Remotion |
| SEO Specialist | Titles, descriptions, tags, thumbnails | Claude + keyword tools |
| Quality Lead | Pressure test + final approval | Multi-model review |

**Additional agents activate as channel grows:**
- Month 2: Shorts Agent, Thumbnail Designer, Newsletter Strategist
- Month 4: Social Media Manager, Analytics VP, Monetization VP
- Month 6+: Full 33-agent council for scaled production

**Pipeline:** Research → Script → Voiceover → Edit → SEO → Quality Gate → Publish

**Quality Gate:** Every video goes through multi-model pressure test (Claude + ChatGPT + Gemini + Grok). Must score 9+/10 from ALL models or loop back with synthesized feedback. Maximum 5 revision cycles.

### 8.2 Audience Feedback Loop

- **Comments Analysis:** Weekly review of top comments, questions, and criticism (automated sentiment analysis)
- **Community Polls:** Monthly "What should we cover next?" polls in Community tab
- **Retention Analytics:** Scene-by-scene retention analysis after every video — identify exactly where viewers drop off and why
- **A/B Title/Thumbnail Testing:** Swap underperformers at 48-hour mark
- **Monthly Strategy Review:** Compare actual metrics vs. projections, adjust content mix and publishing cadence based on data

## STRATEGY PRINCIPLES

1. **YouTube-first.** Master one platform before expanding.
2. **Trust over transactions.** Only monetize what we genuinely use and believe in.
3. **Stories over lectures.** People remember protagonists, not bullet points.
4. **Data over assumptions.** Every decision validated by metrics within 30 days.
5. **Lean scaling.** Start with 6 agents, add complexity only when volume demands it.
6. **Quality gate, always.** Nothing ships below 9/10. The pressure test is non-negotiable.
