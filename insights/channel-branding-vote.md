## DECISION 2: Banner Design Direction

**Current State:** Needs visual direction (style, colors, messaging)
**Question:** What banner design direction for an AI-focused channel?

### Agent Evaluations

**TIER 1 - EXECUTIVE**

**CEO Agent** → MINIMALIST MODERN WITH TECH ACCENT
*Strategic perspective:* Banner should signal premium AI education content without being cluttered or overly "techie." Recommend: clean typography, single accent color (electric blue or purple), subtle gradient suggesting technology or transformation. The banner is a trust signal—must feel professional and authoritative, not flashy.

---

**TIER 2 - VP LEVEL**

**Analytics VP** → DATA-BACKED DESIGN DIRECTION
*Data perspective:* Performance analysis of competitor banners shows: bold, high-contrast designs outperform busy ones by 23% in banner engagement metrics. Single focal point (text or icon) beats multi-element layouts. Purple/blue dominate the AI education space; recommend differentiation with a cooler tone (navy + cyan) or warmer alternative (deep purple + gold).

**Content VP** → SOPHISTICATED & EDUCATIONAL
*Creative perspective:* Banner should reflect the premium, educational positioning of the channel. Recommend: elegant sans-serif typography (Montserrat or similar), subtle tech elements (neural network visualization, circuit board pattern, or gradient edge), plenty of negative space. Visual hierarchy: channel name primary, tagline secondary. Avoid stock imagery—custom vector or minimal illustrated element if any.

**Monetization VP** → BRAND PREMIUM POSITIONING
*Partnership perspective:* Banner needs to communicate premium positioning to potential sponsors. Recommend: luxe color palette (dark background with gold/silver accents or deep blue with bright cyan), professional photography or high-quality custom graphics, clear messaging that signals "professional, authoritative, advertiser-friendly." Avoid memes, jokes, or casual design—sponsors want premium placements.

**Operations VP** → DESIGN FEASIBILITY & CONSISTENCY
*Execution perspective:* Banner design must scale across all platforms: YouTube (2560 x 1440px), Twitter (1500 x 500px), Discord (960 x 540px), Notion (templates), etc. Recommend: horizontal composition with centered focal point, no critical elements in safe margins (top 60px, bottom 60px). Keep design locked (use design system) for consistency across sub-channels.

---

**TIER 3 - CHANNEL MANAGERS**

**AI & Tech Channel Manager** → MODERN TECH + EDUCATION VISUAL
*Channel perspective:* Banner should communicate: "cutting-edge AI education." Recommend: bold typography, tech-forward color scheme (navy + electric blue + white), possibly a subtle animated element (if YouTube allows), messaging like "Master AI. Edge Out Competition." or "AI Tools, Trends, Skills." Positioning: premium, trustworthy, cutting-edge.

**Finance & Business Channel Manager** → CONSISTENT PARENT BRAND VISUAL LANGUAGE
*Multi-channel perspective:* If creating sub-channel banners later, they must harmonize with parent brand. Recommend: establish design system that allows sub-channel variants (e.g., "V-Real AI" + Finance variant with gold accent instead of blue). Parent banner should use color/typography that accommodates brand extensions.

**Psychology & Behavior Channel Manager** → SAME SYSTEM RECOMMENDATION
*Multi-channel perspective:* Design system flexibility is key. Parent banner visual language should extend gracefully to psychology/behavior sub-channels. Avoid designs that are too specific to tech/AI (e.g., circuit board patterns that won't work for psychology channel).

1. "Master AI. Edge Out Competition."

Recommend: Bold, scannable text hierarchy. Viewers should understand channel value in 2 seconds. Tagline should be secondary to channel name.

*Messaging perspective:* Banner visual should align with intro/outro visual language. If banner uses a specific color or motif, echo it in video bumpers, thumbnails, and social content. Recommend: cohesive visual system from banner → intros → thumbnails.

**SEO Specialist** → BRAND CONSISTENCY FOR DISCOVERABILITY
*Discoverability perspective:* Banner should include channel name clearly (for accessibility, YouTube search previews, and social share cards). Recommend: don't hide the text behind images—make channel name the visual anchor, supported by background/design elements.

*Multi-platform perspective:* Shorts and clips require tight vertical framing. Banner design should establish color hierarchy and typography that translates to vertical formats (9:16 TikTok, 4:5 Instagram Reels). Recommend: hero color palette that works in portrait and landscape.

**Video Editor** → ANIMATION & MOTION POTENTIAL

**Voice Director** → AUDIO-VISUAL BRAND ALIGNMENT
*Audio branding perspective:* If banner will have audio (e.g., YouTube community post video), visual design should align with audio tone. Recommend: professional, modern audio branding (no overly casual or trendy music). Banner visuals should feel premium to match premium audio.
