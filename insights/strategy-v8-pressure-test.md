## 1. CHANNEL IDENTITY

**Name:** V-Real AI (@VRealAI)
**Tagline:** "You're not paranoid. You're observant."
**Voice:** Julian — Deep, rich, mature (ElevenLabs Multilingual v2). Julian IS the personality. Consistent voice, consistent perspective, consistent character. Viewers build parasocial trust with Julian's voice and POV, not a face.
**Tone:** BBC/Netflix documentary. Cinematic. Intimate. Urgent but empowering.

**Core Positioning:** V-Real AI is the insider's guide to the AI revolution — specifically for **marketing professionals and solopreneurs** who need to adapt or lose their competitive edge. Documentary-quality storytelling about real transformations in the marketing/business world.

**The Parasocial Element:** Faceless channels struggle with trust. Our solution: Julian becomes a recognizable character — with opinions, recurring references, callbacks to previous episodes, admitted mistakes, and a consistent worldview. Think Anthony Bourdain's voiceover style: you never needed to see him cook to trust his taste. Julian's voice IS the brand personality.

**What We're NOT:** Generic AI news, hustle-culture motivation, academic lectures, clickbait without substance.

## 3. CONTENT STRATEGY

### 3.1 Content Mix — Hybrid Model (Protagonist-Proof)

| Format | % | Protagonist Required? | Example |
|--------|---|----------------------|---------|
| **Stories** (transformation narratives) | 30% | Yes | "She Lost Her Design Career in 20 Minutes — Then Tripled Her Income" |
| **Breakdowns** (tool/trend analysis) | 40% | No — evergreen | "Claude vs GPT-4o vs Gemini: The Real Benchmark Nobody's Showing You" |
| **Playbooks** (tactical how-to) | 30% | No — evergreen | "The $50/Month AI Stack That Replaced a $5,000 Team" |

**Why 30/40/30 not 50/30/20:** Previous versions relied too heavily on protagonist stories (50%). At 3 videos/week = 156/year, that would require 78 verified protagonist stories annually — unsustainable. This mix requires only ~47 stories/year while 70% of content is evergreen and self-sourced.

### 3.2 Protagonist Sourcing System

**Pipeline (targeting 5-6 new stories/month, maintaining 8-week buffer):**

1. **Community Submissions:** "Share your AI story" form in every video description + pinned community post. As audience grows, this becomes the primary source.
2. **AI Community Partnerships:** Formal relationships with 3-5 AI bootcamps, SaaS companies (Make.com, Jasper, Copy.ai), and professional communities (r/marketing, Marketing Twitter) who surface customer success stories in exchange for exposure.
3. **LinkedIn Outreach:** Direct message marketing professionals posting about AI transitions (3-5 per week)
4. **Public Case Studies:** When sourcing is lean, use publicly documented stories from company blogs, conference talks, and published interviews (with attribution, no consent needed for public figures/stories).

**Verification Protocol:** Every original story requires (a) verifiable details (company type, timeframe, tool used), (b) consent for anonymized storytelling, (c) revenue figures as ranges only. All stories use "let's call her [name]" framing.

**Backup Plan:** If story pipeline runs thin in any given month, shift that week's story slot to a breakdown or playbook. Content calendar has 2-week flex built in.

### 3.3 Video Structure (8-12 minutes)

- **Hook (0:00-0:30):** 3-second stop-scroll + bold claim + knowledge gap
- **Act 1 — Setup (0:30-2:00):** Stakes, context, "why you should care TODAY"
- **Act 2 — Core (2:00-9:00):** Story/breakdown with visual cues, data, B-roll
- **Act 3 — Payoff (9:00-11:00):** Resolution, key takeaway, transformation
- **CTA (11:00-12:00):** Subscribe + specific next action

### 3.4 YouTube Shorts Strategy (From Day 1)

- Extract 3-5 clip-worthy moments from each long-form video
- Standalone Shorts: 2x/week original micro-stories (60-90 sec)
- Target: Daily Shorts output (mix of clips + originals)
- Purpose: Algorithm discovery → funnel to long-form → subscriber conversion
- Each Short ends with: "Full story on the channel"

**Pipeline:** Research → Script → Voiceover → Edit → SEO → Quality Gate → Publish

**Quality Gate:** Multi-model pressure test (Claude + ChatGPT + Gemini + Grok). Score 9+/10 from ALL models or revision loop. Max 5 cycles.

### 8.2 Audience Feedback Loop

- **Comments Analysis:** Weekly review — top questions become future video topics
- **Community Polls:** Monthly "What should we cover next?"
- **Retention Analytics:** Scene-by-scene analysis after every video — identify drop-off points
- **A/B Testing:** Title/thumbnail swap at 48 hours if below CTR targets
- **Monthly Strategy Review:** Actuals vs. projections → adjust content mix, cadence, and monetization strategy
